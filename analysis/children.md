**Headline finding (honest):** on this file the classical 5th-house/Jupiter apparatus does **not** beat the base rate for the childless-vs-parent split, because *childlessness here is overwhelmingly a marriage outcome, not a 5th-house outcome.* At least 8 of the 23 childless are explicitly unmarried or not-yet-married (752, 761, 765, 813, 819, 822, 825, 827 — six of these say so in REASON/FACT), and several more are marriage-afflicted (789 married-no-issue, 798 childless-after-marriage, 801 live-in, 790/773 adopted). The fertility indicators (benefic in 5th, exalted/own Jupiter) fire *just as often* for these childless-unmarried natives as for parents — so they cannot separate the two. This coupling is the single most important thing to know for this topic.

## Diagnostic factors (ranked)

1. **Marital status (dominant, exogenous).** "Childless" ≈ "unmarried / broken marriage." The clearest demonstrations: **772** has Jupiter in *its own sign in the 5th house* (JuSgH5 own) — a textbook putra yoga — yet is childless (Manglik, marriage blocked). **753 & 811** have Jupiter *exalted* yet childless. **789** has 5th-lord Saturn *in own sign in the 5th* (SaCpH5 own) yet married-4-years-no-child. Conversely, afflicted 5th houses still deliver children when the person is married: **788** (Jupiter debilitated) has two; **826** (Saturn+Rahu in 5th) has three. → *Resolve marriage first; only then read the 5th.*

2. **Jupiter (putrakaraka) debilitated → caps children low.** Best genuine sub-rule in the file. Debilitated-Jupiter natives: 767, 773, 788, 790, 798, 808, 813, 820. **7 of 8 have ≤1 child** (five childless, two one-child); only 788 reaches two. Strong, clean signal.

3. **5th lord in a dusthana (6/8/12) or debilitated → leans childless.** Supports many childless (753 Ju→12, 758 Sa deb→8, 765 Ve→6, 767 Ju deb→6, 781 Ve deb→9, 794 Ma→8, 796 Su→12, 798 Ve→6, 813 Mo deb→8). BUT it is noisy: it also fires for parents (755 Ju→8 has two; 788 Ma→6 has two; 802 Ju→12 has two; 803 Sa deb→7 has two sons; 826 Ma→8 has three). Useful as a *tilt*, not a gate.

4. **Benefic (Jupiter/Venus/Mercury) physically in the 5th → children, IF married.** 754, 779, 804, 816, 823 all have a benefic in the 5th and 2 children. But 752, 772, 822 also have a benefic in the 5th and are childless (all unmarried/marriage-blocked). So: 4/7 → children; the 3 misses are all the marriage problem again.

5. **Jupiter exalted/own or 5th-lord own/exalted → nudges toward "two."** Among parents this tracks 2-child families (774, 802, 816, 823, 779). Standalone it is unreliable (753, 801, 811 have it and are childless-unmarried).

6. **Malefics in the 5th → NOT a childless marker here.** Rahu in the 5th especially co-occurs with *having* children: 771, 793, 795, 808, 823, 826, 830 all have Rahu (or Sat+Rahu) in the 5th and all are parents (two of them the largest families). Saturn/Ketu in the 5th is mixed. Do **not** read a malefic in the 5th as childlessness on this data.

7. **Sons vs daughters — sons dominate; predict "at least a son."** Of ~22 gendered parent-outcomes, only **two are daughters-only** (802 = two daughters, 820 = one daughter). "At least one son" is correct ~20/22 ≈ **91%**. The two daughter-only charts share: 5th house in a **female/watery sign** (Cancer for 802, Pisces for 820), Jupiter as the operative karaka in a female sign (Ju exa in Cancer H12 for 802; Ju in Capricorn/Pisces axis for 820), and **no Sun or Mars sitting in the 5th**. Son-heavy charts more often show a male sign on the 5th and/or Sun/Mars there (803 Sun in Aquarius-5th → two sons; 815 Sagittarius-5th → two sons; 823 Gemini-5th → two sons). This is a weak 2-case pattern; the reliable move is simply to bet "son(s)."

## Decision procedure

For a new chart:

1. **Marriage gate first.** Judge marriage independently (7th house/lord, Venus, Manglik, dashas). If marriage looks unlikely or badly broken → predict **CHILDLESS** and stop. (This carries most of the childless bucket. Do NOT let a strong 5th house override an unmarried verdict — 772/753/811 prove it.)
2. If Jupiter is **debilitated** → predict **CHILDLESS or ONE** (cap low; 7/8).
3. Else count from the 5th:
   - Predict **TWO** if a benefic (Jupiter/Venus/Mercury) sits in the 5th, **or** Jupiter is exalted/own, **or** the 5th lord is own/exalted.
   - Otherwise predict **ONE**.
4. Predict **THREE+** only if the 5th holds a malefic cluster (Saturn+Rahu, or Mars) *and* the 5th lord is in the 8th (both large families, 795 & 826, fit this).
5. **Sex of child:** default to "at least one son." Lean "daughter(s)" only if the 5th house is a female/watery sign, Jupiter (in a female sign) is the sole progeny driver, and neither Sun nor Mars is in the 5th.

## Back-test result

Running the chart-only version of the procedure (steps 2–4, since the marriage gate can't be scored from the given fields):

- **Exact bucket: 17/47 (36%).** — *Below* the 49% base rate of "always say childless." The classical count-machinery actively mis-fires because it upgrades unmarried natives with good 5th houses to "two."
- **Within-one-bucket: 33/47 (70%).**
- **Base rate to beat: 23/47 (49%) exact** (always-childless).

Exact-bucket misses (14 outside ±1 bucket): 752 (pred 2, childless-unmarried), 755 (pred childless via Ju→8, has 2), 757 (pred 2, childless), 772 (pred 2, Jupiter-own-in-5th but unmarried), 779 (pred childless via Mo→12, but Ju-exa-in-5th gives 2), 788 (pred childless, deb-Jupiter exception, has 2), 789 (pred 2, married-no-issue), 795 (pred 1, has 3), 801 (pred 2, live-in childless), 802 (pred childless via Ju→12, has 2), 803 (pred childless via Sa-deb, has 2 sons), 811 (pred 2, childless-unmarried), 822 (pred 2, childless-unmarried), 826 (pred childless via Ma→8, has 3).

**Interpretation:** nearly every large miss is one of two failure modes — (a) a strong 5th house whose native is unmarried (752, 772, 811, 822, 801, 789), or (b) a dusthana 5th-lord whose married native had children anyway (755, 802, 803, 826). Both are the marriage-coupling problem. The one rule that survives back-test cleanly is **debilitated-Jupiter → ≤1 child (7/8).**

## Highest-signal rules

1. **Childless ⇐ unmarried.** ≥8/23 childless are explicitly unmarried/not-yet-married (752, 761, 765, 813, 819, 822, 825, 827); resolving marriage first is worth more than any 5th-house rule. (772 = Jupiter own-sign in 5th, still childless.)
2. **Debilitated Jupiter → ≤1 child.** 7/8 (767, 773, 790, 798, 813 childless; 808, 820 one child; only 788 = two).
3. **Rahu in the 5th ≠ childless — it co-occurs with parenthood.** 771, 793, 795, 808, 823, 826, 830 all have children; 795 & 826 are the two largest families. Ignore the classical "Rahu spoils the 5th" here.
4. **Benefic (Ju/Ve/Me) in the 5th → two children, *conditional on marriage*.** 754, 779, 804, 816, 823 → two; the exceptions (752, 772, 822) are all marriage-blocked.
5. **Sons dominate:** "at least one son" ≈ 20/22 (91%). Daughters-only (802, 820) only when the 5th is a female sign, Jupiter is the sole driver, and no Sun/Mars in the 5th.
6. **Three+ children:** both cases (795, 826) have a malefic cluster in the 5th (Sat+Rahu / Mars) with the 5th lord in the 8th — a Rahu-charged, malefic-loaded 5th, not a "clean" one.

## Caveats

- **Marriage is the confound.** Half the natives are childless, and most of that is marital, not reproductive-chart, driven. Any 5th-house rule tested here inherits noise from marriage. A properly staged model (marriage → then children) would score far better, but marriage can't be scored from the fields supplied, so the honest chart-only number is exact 36% / within-bucket 70%.
- **D7 (Saptamsha) is absent.** Several REASON lines (795, 804, 808) explicitly decide count/sex from the D7 — e.g. 808 "in D7 the 5th lord Mars is in the ascendant → son." Only D9/D10 are provided, so those calls are unrecoverable here and the 5th house/lord + Jupiter are doing all the work.
- **Thin cells:** three-plus is only n=2, and daughters-only is only n=2 — both patterns are suggestive, not established.
- **Adopted-son cases (773, 790)** were bucketed as childless (no biological issue); if counted as "one child" the childless bucket drops to 21.
- **"Currently childless" ambiguity:** 819 will become a father "in September" and 813 is "not thinking about children yet" — both are childless *now* but chart-fertile, inflating the childless bucket with people who are simply early.
