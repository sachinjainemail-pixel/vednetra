## SIBLINGS

**n = 34 natives.** Answers are sibling counts (brothers B / sisters S). Two structural facts dominate and must be stated up front:

- **Only-child is untestable here: 0/34 natives are only children.** Every native has ≥1 sibling. Selection bias — the quiz asks "how many siblings," so only-children don't appear. Any classical "only-child" rule cannot be validated on this data.
- **Include/exclude-native ambiguity is real.** Posters themselves flagged it (765: "unclear whether the question counts the native"; 829: option could read two ways; 764: stated "two sisters one brother" but FACT is one elder sister + one younger brother). This alone puts a ceiling on exact-count scoring.

Skew distribution (B vs S, from the stated CORRECT answer):

| Skew | Count | Share |
|---|---|---|
| more brothers (B>S) | 16 | 47% |
| more sisters (S>B) | 11 | 32% |
| equal (B=S) | 7 | 21% |

Base rates that ARE strong (but are demographic, not astrological):
- **Has ≥1 brother: 31/34 = 91%** (no-brother: 765, 794, 829).
- **Has ≥1 sister: 27/34 = 79%** (no-sister: 781, 789, 795, 802, 814, 826, 827).
- **Predict "more brothers" for everyone: 16/34 = 47%.**

### Diagnostic factors (ranked)

1. **Demographic base rates (above).** By far the most accurate statements available: "you have a brother" is right 91% of the time, "you have a sister" 79%.

2. **Classical "Mars on the 3rd/11th → brothers" FAILS here.** Testing "Mars occupies or aspects (conj / 4th / 7th / 8th aspect) house 3 or 11 → predict brother-lean": it fires on 20/34 charts, correct on only 45% of them, and non-firing charts are non-brother only 50% of the time → **combined 16/34 = 47%, identical to the base rate (zero lift).** Worse, several sister-heavy charts (768, 779, 794, 796, 816, 820) DO have Mars touching 3/11, while strong-brother charts (781 B4, 789, 800, 802) do NOT. Mars-as-brother-karaka does not hold in this sample.

3. **Gendered occupants of houses 3 + 11 — weak but the best available.** Tally male planets (Sun/Mars/Jupiter) vs female (Moon/Venus/Mercury) sitting in the 3rd and 11th; predict skew by majority, default brother-lean on a tie/empty. This reaches **17/34 = 50%** — a marginal +1 over base rate. On the ~44% of charts where 3/11 actually hold gendered planets and give a non-tie call, accuracy rises to ~53% (8/15). Examples that work: 768 (Moon+Mercury in 11th → sisters ✓), 795 (Sun+Mars in 11th → brothers ✓), 816 (Venus in 3rd → sisters ✓), 800/808 (Sun in 11th → brothers ✓).

4. **3rd/11th-lord gender** adds nothing measurable on top of #3.

5. **Weak no-brother hint:** the 3 no-brother charts (765, 794, 829) each have Mars weak — in a dusthana (794 Mars H8), retrograde (829 Mars R), or unrelated to 3/11 (765). n=3, so suggestive only.

### Decision procedure

For a new chart:
1. **Has siblings? → Yes** (this sample is 100% siblings; only-child cannot be diagnosed here).
2. **Has a brother? → Yes** (91% base rate). **Has a sister? → Yes** (79%).
3. **Skew:**
   a. Tally male (Sun/Mars/Jupiter) vs female (Moon/Venus/Mercury) planets in houses **3 and 11**.
   b. Male majority → more brothers; female majority → more sisters; tie or empty → default **more brothers**.
4. **Exact count: decline or give a range.** Do not commit to a number — see back-test.

### Back-test result

- **Skew, exact bucket:** occupant-majority rule (brother-lean default) = **17/34 = 50%**, vs 47% for naive always-brother-lean. Essentially no astrological lift.
- **Skew, brother/non-brother only (2-way):** the Mars-on-3/11 rule = 16/34 = 47%; the occupant rule ~53% on the subset where 3/11 are occupied.
- **Has-a-brother:** 91%. **Has-a-sister:** 79% (both purely base rate).
- **Exact sibling count:** effectively untestable — the 34 natives spread across ~13 distinct count-answers (1 sister … four brothers … four sisters two brothers), 1–3 natives per answer, compounded by the include-native ambiguity. No rule reproduces exact counts above chance.

Representative skew misses (occupant rule): 789 (Mercury+Venus in 3rd predicts sisters, actual 2 brothers), 803 (Moon in 11th predicts sisters, actual brother-lean), 820 & 823 (male occupants predict brothers, actual sister-heavy / mixed), 764/765/772/779/794/830 (empty 3+11 default to brother, actual sister-lean).

### Highest-signal rules

1. **"You have a brother" — 91% (31/34).** The most reliable sibling statement, independent of any chart factor.
2. **"You have a sister" — 79% (27/34).**
3. **Gendered planets in the 3rd+11th nudge the skew, when present:** Sun/Mars/Jupiter majority → brother-lean, Moon/Venus/Mercury majority → sister-lean (~53% on occupied charts). Supported by 768, 795, 796, 800, 808, 816, 829.
4. **Do NOT trust Mars-as-brother-karaka on the 3rd/11th** — it scores at chance (47%) and sometimes reverses.

### Caveats

- **Zero only-children** → the has-siblings-vs-only-child question is unanswerable from this data; report it as a known blind spot, not a solved rule.
- Skew prediction sits at **47–50%**, barely above guessing brother-lean; the honest conclusion is that sibling gender-mix is **near-unpredictable** from these charts.
- Exact counts are unreliable and worsened by the systemic include/exclude-native ambiguity that the posters themselves could not resolve.
- Sample is siblings-only and likely young natives; brother-lean (47%) may partly reflect the poster pool, not a universal astrological law.
